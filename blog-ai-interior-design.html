AI for Interior Design: Space Planning, Client Mood Boards, Project Management, Material Sourcing & Design Analytics

Interior design practices face a compressed margin challenge — clients expect increasingly sophisticated design outcomes, faster visualisation turnaround, and broader material research depth than design teams can deliver at hourly rates that the residential and commercial markets will pay in 2026. The design studios growing margins, not shrinking them, are those using AI to multiply the design team's output capacity — generating mood board directions from a brief in minutes, surfacing specification alternatives across a broader material landscape than any individual designer can hold in memory, automating the project management administration that consumes studio principal time, and researching procurement options across a substantially wider supplier base than traditional relationships and directories can cover.

AI in interior design does not replace the aesthetic judgment, client relationship intelligence, and spatial experience that defines exceptional design — it eliminates the research, administration, and presentation production work that consumes designer time without contributing to the design outcome quality that clients pay for and reference projects demonstrate.
