AI for Biotech: Drug Discovery, Clinical Trials, Genomics, Lab Automation & Regulatory Affairs

Biotech drug development is among the most data-intensive, time-consuming, and capital-expensive processes in the global economy — average drug development timelines of 12-15 years and billion-dollar development costs create enormous pressure to find ways to synthesise scientific data faster, design better trials, and navigate regulatory pathways more efficiently. AI is compressing those timelines by augmenting the scientific and operational workflows that determine how fast a molecule moves from discovery to patients.

The biotech companies gaining competitive advantage in 2026 are those treating AI as a core scientific capability — not a back-office productivity tool — and integrating machine intelligence into discovery, development, and regulatory processes from Day 1.

Six AI biotech workflows

🔬

Drug Discovery & Target Identification

Analyses biological data — protein structures, genomic sequences, literature databases, and target validation datasets — to identify novel drug targets and optimise lead compound selection. ↓40% early discovery cycle time and ↑30% success rate in lead compound identification from AI-assisted target identification versus hypothesis-driven manual literature review and experimental screening alone.

↓ 40% discovery cycle time
🏥

Clinical Trial Design & Recruitment

Optimises clinical trial design — endpoint selection, patient stratification, site selection, and protocol parameters — and accelerates patient recruitment by identifying eligible patients from electronic health records and real-world data. ↓30% clinical trial recruitment time and ↑25% trial completion rate from AI-optimised site selection and patient identification versus traditional recruitment approaches.

↓ 30% trial recruitment time
🧬

Genomic Data Analysis

Processes and interprets genomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic datasets — identifying biomarkers, patient stratification signals, and mechanistic insights that inform patient selection, combination strategies, and precision medicine approaches. ↑50% genomic data analysis throughput from AI-assisted multi-omic data integration versus sequential manual analysis of individual data modalities.

↑ 50% genomic data throughput
🤖

Lab Automation

Orchestrates laboratory workflows — experimental scheduling, instrument coordination, results processing, and quality control — reducing the manual overhead that limits experimental throughput and introduces human error into data collection. ↑35% experimental throughput and ↓25% lab error rate from AI-orchestrated laboratory automation versus manually scheduled and supervised experimental workflows.

↑ 35% experimental throughput
📋

Regulatory Submission Preparation

Compiles clinical data, prepares regulatory document packages, and checks submission completeness against FDA and EMA requirements — reducing the preparation time and submission deficiency rates that delay regulatory review. ↓50% regulatory submission preparation time from AI-assisted document compilation and quality checking versus manual submission preparation by regulatory affairs teams.

↓ 50% regulatory submission prep time
⚙️

Biotech Operations

Automates IP surveillance, patent landscape monitoring, competitive intelligence, and biotech operational workflows — freeing scientific and business teams from information-gathering tasks to focus on high-value scientific and strategic work. ↓45% competitive intelligence gathering time from AI-automated biotech operations workflows that continuously monitor the scientific and commercial landscape.

↓ 45% competitive intel gathering time
